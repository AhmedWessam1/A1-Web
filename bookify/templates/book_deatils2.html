Rich Dad Poor Dad

Category

Nonfiction — Personal Finance / Self-Development. The book focuses on financial literacy, investing, and wealth-building mindset.

Author

Robert T. Kiyosaki — an American entrepreneur, investor, and author known for his teachings on financial education and independence. He is best known for the "Rich Dad" series of personal finance books.

Published

First published in 1997.

Available

Description

Rich Dad Poor Dad tells the story of Robert Kiyosaki growing up with two father figures: his biological father ("Poor Dad"), who was highly educated but struggled financially, and his best friend's father ("Rich Dad"), who was less formally educated but became wealthy through investing and entrepreneurship.

The book contrasts the mindsets of the two dads and highlights key lessons about money, such as the importance of financial education, building assets instead of liabilities, and making money work for you rather than working for money.

It emphasizes concepts like passive income, investing in real estate and businesses, and developing a mindset focused on financial independence instead of relying solely on a traditional job.

Key Lessons

  • The rich don’t work for money — they make money work for them.
  • Assets put money in your pocket; liabilities take money out.
  • Financial education is more important than formal education.
  • Investing and entrepreneurship are key to building wealth.
  • Overcoming fear and taking risks is essential for success.
